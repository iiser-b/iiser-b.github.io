Sign Language Workshop

Sign language is used to communicate with a vast spectrum of people with special needs. While it is quintessential to ensure we can communicate with them, most of us are hardly acquainted with it. ESIC, in collaboration with the Sign Language Research and Training Centre of India (ISLRTC) of the Govt of India, organized an awareness session on Indian Sign Languages.

Rant Session

On World Mental health day, we made an anonymous platform for people to share their feelings and thoughts. It involved incidents that gave them hope or made them feel better or simply smile an expression of gratitude for someone or something, and rant about absolutely anything. We wanted to make people realize that they aren’t alone in this, and other people are going through something similar, if not the same.

Think Globally, Act Locally

A Workshop on Waste Management and Cleanliness.
This workshop was co-organized by Forengers on the occasion of Swacchata Pakhwada. This workshop had two aspects, the interaction part, and activity over the week. We tried to gain a new perspective on making our lives more sustainable through mindfulness and proactiveness through this initiative. We also had live demonstrations on how to upcycle waste and a demonstration on making your own bird feeder. We also observed a "Zero Waste Week" in between the two sessions.

Plantation Drive

As a part of our collab series for Jal Shakti Abhiyan, on Independence Day, we collaborated with EBSB for an exciting event- Plantation Drive. This event aimed to educate people about the importance of growing trees and spread the message that tree plantation is not just something that should be done; instead, it is a necessity, the urgent need of the hour. Planting trees is crucial to protect our environment against air pollution and global warming.

Talk on Water Conservation

Under the initiative of Jal Shakti Abhiyan’s “Catch the rain initiative,” we arranged a talk by Mr. Vishwanath S, also known as the Zenrainman of India, a renowned water conservation expert. In this talk, he touched upon how livelihoods can be integrated into the solution to water scarcity and pollution. Water conservation techniques like- Rainwater harvesting, lake rejuvenation, and wastewater reuse were also discussed using case studies from the individual, community, to the metropolis level.

Backyard Nature Challenge

This challenge aimed to create awareness among people. It also intended to engage people and motivate them to find and document wildlife in their cities but with an exciting challenge; Which state in India can document the most species? The primary purpose of this event is to embrace the healing powers of nature and celebrate the biological diversity of India. We attempted to make people more conscious about their surrounding environment through this community event by promoting nature walks, sustainable lifestyles, and nature conservation.

Pride month Talk

On the occasion of Pride Month, we organized a talk “To Be or Not to Be a Scientist” by Sayantan Datta (they/them), who are a queer-trans science journalist. The Talk revolved around the queer-trans people in Indian science & academia, what science institutions can do to improve the representation of queer-trans people in science, and what it may mean for science to be queer.
